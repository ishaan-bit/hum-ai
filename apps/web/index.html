Hum AI

Your voice, once a day.

A private, standardized 12-second hum as a daily ritual. Voice-first affective modeling that runs on-device, surfaces reflective signals, and never tells you what you have.

This page is a preview placeholder — the product UI is not built yet. The intelligence core lives in the packages/* workspaces. Nothing on this page is a finished or validated product.

What it is & what it is not

What it is

  • A private daily voice ritual — one hum, about 12 seconds
  • Voice-first affective signal modeling, on-device
  • Reflective markers and longitudinal patterns over time
  • Local-first: raw audio stays on your device by default
  • A research architecture in progress

What it is not

  • Not a medical device or diagnostic tool
  • Does not produce a diagnosis, ever
  • Not clinically validated
  • Not FDA-cleared or regulated
  • Not a finished or shipped product

How it works (when built)

  1. 1 Record a ~12-second hum — a short, consistent vocal gesture, nothing more.
  2. 2 Extract audio features entirely on-device. Raw audio never moves.
  3. 3 Gate quality — if the hum is too noisy or short, ask again.
  4. 4 Run voice-affect experts (speech emotion, text emotion, optionally face expression) and fuse signals with calibrated, capped confidence.
  5. 5 Compare against your personal baseline — no population norms imposed on you.
  6. 6 Return plain-language reflective copy. No raw scores shown. No diagnosis. No clinical language.

Privacy

Raw audio never leaves your device by default. Only derived, numerical features can sync — and the sync payload is validated at the type level to assert no raw audio fields are present (assertNoRawAudioFields). Your hum stays yours. Camera input is not part of this design.

Architecture & status

The intelligence core — audio feature extraction, quality gating, domain classification, expert models, fusion engine, personalization, relapse detection, intervention engine, and safety-language rendering — is implemented as a set of typed TypeScript packages under packages/* in this monorepo.

The hum-only voice core is now real and runnable locally: a pure-TypeScript DSP feature extractor feeds the quality gate, domain classifier, experts, fusion, personalization and safety-language layers end to end. Run npm run demo:voice to drive synthetic hums through the whole pipeline (no microphone, no camera). It is still research-stage and non-clinical — deterministic signal processing, not a trained or validated model.

The browser client (this surface) is intentionally not built in this pass. Wiring a real capture surface → pipeline → display is a later step. This placeholder exists so the Vercel project can be linked and previewed without fabricating a product page.
